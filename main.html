Legal Issues within ICT & Entertainment

There are many problems today in the entertainment industry. Believe it or not, a majority of these problems are legal issues! But first we must find out what a legal issue is in terms of ICT. A legal issue is a problem created when ICT is used in a way in which conflicts with the law. When we put this into the entertainment industry, we get issues that range from pirating, stealing content to copyright infringement, torrenting etc.

Millions of people are affected when technology is misused in a way in which conflicts with the law. Pirating programs and forms of entertainment have been around since cavemen (Maybe not that long). In the entertainment industry, there are many stakeholder that are affected when ICT is misused such as, movie directors, song artists, video game developers and only a few of the people. When legal issues are made through illegally downloading or using someone's content, money is lost on the creator's side even though they should be earning more. This is why legal issues can be a hassle and big problems for areas like entertainment.

Pirating is a way of stealing someone’s content and making money of it. This has been done many times with movies and music. People have videotaped or recorded these forms of entertainment and put it online for the world to see. This may sound great because who doesn’t like free movies and music? But really it is illegal and unfair.

Torrenting, though can be something good and useful can also be illegal at times. Torrenting can be used in a way to distribute games, programs and other forms of digital entertainment through peer to peer file sharing. Peer to peer can be more difficult to track as many components are shared across different computers making it hard to minimise the problem of copyright infringement and pirating.
